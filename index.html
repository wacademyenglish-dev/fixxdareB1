Directive 01

Welcome, Guide

This class is dark, mature, and moral.

Your Intermediate to Upper-Intermediate (B1/B2) student will explore the fine line between a lawyer who defends the law by day, and a vigilante who breaks it by night.

Grammar Targets:

  • 👁️ Sense Verbs: Describing the world without sight (Look, Sound, Smell, Feel + like).
  • 📰 Passive Voice: Crime reporting and hiding the subject (He was murdered).

Grammar Focus

Sense Verbs

Matt Murdock is blind. We must describe the world using other senses.

1. Verb + Adjective

"He sounds angry." / "The fabric feels soft."

2. Verb + LIKE + Noun

"It smells like rain." / "It tastes like blood."

Interactive Challenge

Blindfolded

Complete the sentences using non-visual clues. Click to reveal.

1. I hear a siren. It ___ the police.

> sounds like

Reason:

Verb + LIKE + Noun (the police).

🗣️ Prompt:

What does your favorite song sound like to you?

2. The fabric is soft. It ___ expensive.

> feels

Reason:

Verb + Adjective (expensive).

🗣️ Prompt:

Have you ever bought something just because it felt good?

3. Is something burning? It ___ smoke.

> smells like

Reason:

Verb + LIKE + Noun (smoke).

🗣️ Prompt:

What is a smell that brings back a strong memory for you?

Grammar Module

The Passive Voice

Why do we use the Passive Voice in Crime Reporting? To focus on the victim or the crime, not the criminal.

Active (Focus on who did it)

"The Kingpin controls the city."

Passive (Focus on the victim/action)

"The city is controlled by the Kingpin."

Grammar Module

Structure

Object + Verb To Be + Past Participle

"He was murdered."

"The money was stolen."

"The suspect has been arrested."
